## Overview

AUREA is a website builder shaped around one narrow use case: helping designers publish portfolios faster without getting trapped in heavy setup and repeated layout work.

## Problem

Many portfolio tools either prioritize generic templates or require too much manual configuration. Designers often need something between a blank canvas and a rigid site builder.

## Research

I looked at how portfolio creators struggle with the same tasks repeatedly:

- setting visual rhythm and spacing
- making work pages feel consistent
- handling responsive layout details
- shipping a public site without fighting the stack

That research pushed the product toward faster defaults and clearer structure.

## Architecture

The product was built as a frontend-heavy application with modular page composition, reusable layout patterns, and a backend layer for persistence and publishing workflows.

## Implementation

My work focused on frontend architecture, product direction, and making the editing experience feel lightweight. I worked on reusable interface patterns, content flow, and the path from draft to published portfolio.

## Challenges

The main challenge was balancing flexibility with clarity. Too much freedom turns the builder into work. Too little freedom makes every portfolio feel the same.

## Lessons Learned

Creative tools need opinionated structure. The best product decisions often came from removing options rather than adding them.
